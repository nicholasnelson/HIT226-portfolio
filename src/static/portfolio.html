FancyFan

The FancyFan project is the result of the combination of too many summer days spent sweltering at my computer and an enjoyment of novelty over-engineered solutions.

It incorporates an ESP8266 microprocessor / wifi chip, custom power supply board, motion and temperature sensors, three 120mm PC case fans, four status LEDs, and two very satisfyingly clicky control buttons, all housed in a 330mm hand-crafted brushed aluminium case with a custom wooden control panel.

It's too big to fit nicely on my desk... but that's not really the point.

bf.js

bf.js is another toy I wrote over a few hours one weekend after reading about esoteric languages on Google. It is an in-browser interpreter for one of the better known esoteric languages.

The language itself uses only 8 commands, represented by 8 different characters: < > + - , , [ ]

The interpreter allows either standard or step-by-step execution, and displays the state of memory cells on either side of the pointer position making it easier to troubleshoot programs.
